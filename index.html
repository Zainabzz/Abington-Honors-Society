About

A Community of Engaged Scholars

Penn State Abington offers two honors pathways for academically exceptional students — the Abington Honors Program and the university-wide Schreyer Honors College. Both provide opportunities to take honors courses, pursue original research, and be part of an active scholarly community.

Academic Distinction

Honors graduates receive special recognition at commencement and a notation on their official Penn State transcript.

Research Opportunities

Work one-on-one with a faculty mentor on an ACURA undergraduate research project and present your findings at a campus symposium.

Study Abroad

Earn honors credits through approved international study experiences and add a global perspective to your honors portfolio.

Honors Community

Join networking events, colloquia, service projects, and social gatherings with fellow honors scholars throughout the year.

ACURA Research Guide

A step-by-step guide to applying for and completing undergraduate research through the Abington Center for Undergraduate Research and Artistry.

1

Find a Faculty Mentor

Browse faculty research profiles on the Abington website and reach out to professors whose work aligns with your interests. Email them a brief introduction and your academic background.

2

Submit an ACURA Application

Complete the ACURA application form available from the Honors Coordinator. Applications are typically due in early October (fall) and early February (spring).

3

Complete Your Research Project

Work with your mentor over the semester on your agreed-upon project. Maintain a research log and check in with your mentor at least bi-weekly.

4

Present at the Poster Fair

At the end of the semester, present your findings at the ACURA Poster Fair. This fulfills your honors research requirement and earns you honors credit.

Student Expectations

Review the ACURA student expectations before beginning your project: ACURA Student Expectations.

Honors Option Contract

Use an Honors Option Contract to earn honors credit in any standard course by completing an enriched assignment with your instructor.

How It Works

An Honors Option Contract is an agreement between you and your instructor to complete additional, enriched work in a non-honors course. Upon successful completion, the course counts toward your honors credit total.

Eligibility

Any honors student maintaining a 3.40 GPA may request an Honors Option Contract. The instructor must agree and co-sign the contract. The instructor must be a full-time faculty member of Penn State Abington (title of Teaching Professor or Professor). Contracts are limited to 3 credits per semester.

Submission Deadlines

Contracts must be submitted to the Honors Coordinator by the end of the second week of the semester. Late submissions will not be accepted.

Campus Program

Abington Honors Program

A campus-based honors pathway for academically motivated students at Penn State Abington, emphasizing research, coursework, and community.

Credits Required

12 honors credits total across your degree

GPA Requirement

Minimum 3.40 GPA to enter and maintain standing

Research

At least 3 credits via ACURA undergraduate research

Recognition

Transcript notation + recognition at commencement

How to complete the Abington Honors Program

Students must complete a combination of 12 credits in honors courses (designated with an "H"), 400 level course taken during your first 4 semesters of undergraduate study, and activities. Activities may include ACURA research projects, study abroad, and individual honors options for specific courses. Students are also required to attend at least one Honors event during their study at Abington.

Who Should Apply

The Abington Honors Program is ideal for students who want to pursue deeper academic engagement while remaining at the Abington campus. It's designed to be flexible enough to fit any major and complement students' professional and personal goals.

How to Join

Qualified students are invited to apply upon acceptance for admission to Penn State Abington and during orientation. Current students may apply before completing 60 credits. To be eligible to apply, students must have a 3.6 cumulative GPA. To remain in the program through graduation, students must maintain a 3.4 Cumulative GPA.

University Program

Schreyer Honors College

Penn State's prestigious university-wide honors college offering scholarships, priority registration, and an honors thesis requirement.

Credits Required

36 honors credits, including an honors thesis

GPA Requirement

Minimum 3.40 GPA; highly competitive admission

Scholarship

Merit-based scholarship for qualifying Schreyer Scholars

Scope

The Schreyer Honors College is available to students at University Park, Abington, Altoona, Berks, Brandywine, Erie, or Harrisburg.

The Honors Thesis

All Schreyer Scholars complete a substantial honors thesis under the guidance of a faculty mentor. The thesis is an original piece of scholarship or creative work, typically 40–100 pages, that represents the culmination of your undergraduate research.

Admission

First year students should apply to the Schreyer Honors College at the same time they apply to Penn State. Current students with a GPA of 3.7 or higher can apply during the spring semester of their first or second year of college.

Additional Benefits

Schreyer Scholars enjoy priority course registration, access to university-wide honors events, a dedicated advising network, and recognition at graduation from both the Schreyer Honors College and Penn State Abington.
